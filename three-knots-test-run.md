The innkeeper, Mara Thornwood, tends the bar. Tension hangs in the air—the village has been troubled by nightmares, cold spots, and sightings of a pale figure near the old tomb road. Recently, a group of treasure-seekers broke into the tomb north of the village; within days the haunting began.

The firelight flickers across her weathered face as she sets down her cloth. "Another traveler," she says quietly, glancing toward a corner table where a hooded figure sits hunched over an untouched ale. "You picked a strange time to visit Three Knots, friend. The whole village has been... unsettled." Before you can respond, footsteps approach from behind. A woman in a dark cloak rises from the corner table—Brenna, her face drawn with exhaustion and fear. "You look like someone who might be brave enough to help," she says, her voice trembling slightly. "My child hasn't slept properly in days. None of us have. It started when those fool treasure-hunters broke into the old tomb north of here. Now we have nightmares, cold spots in our homes, and people claim they've seen a pale figure walking the tomb road at night." She leans closer, desperation in her eyes. "The mayor's offering good coin—100 gold pieces and a fine weapon—to anyone who can end this curse. Will you go to the tomb and stop whatever those fools awakened?"
[Player] I cross the room to speak to Brenna
[GM] You rise from the bar stool and cross the common room to Brenna's table. Up close, you can see the toll the haunting has taken—dark circles under her eyes, hands that shake slightly as she grips her ale mug. The firelight casts long shadows across her gaunt face. "Thank the gods," she whispers as you approach. "Someone who doesn't just look away when I mention the tomb." She gestures to the empty chair across from her. "Please, sit. Let me tell you what's been happening." She leans forward, lowering her voice. "My little one wakes up screaming every night, talking about 'the cold king' coming for him. Others in the village report the same dreams. Windows frost over on warm nights. And just yesterday, old Henrik swore he saw a figure in royal robes standing at the crossroads—pale as bone, with eyes like winter stars." Her grip tightens on the mug. "Those treasure-seekers broke the seal on the tomb three days ago. The haunting began that very night. Whatever they disturbed down there, it won't rest until someone puts it back in the ground properly." She looks at you with desperate hope. "Will you help us? The mayor promises his finest weapon from the old armory and 100 gold pieces to whoever can end this nightmare."
[Player] I leave the inn and head northward
[GM] You push through the inn's heavy oak door into the cool evening air. The village of Three Knots spreads before you—a handful of timber houses with shuttered windows, most already dark despite the early hour. The streets are empty; even the usual sounds of evening activity are muted. Following Brenna's directions, you head north through the village center. The cobblestone path gives way to packed earth as you leave the last houses behind. The narrow trail winds through bare, rolling hills dotted with scraggly trees that seem to lean away from your destination. The air grows noticeably colder with each step. Your breath begins to mist, though the evening isn't that chill. The silence is oppressive—no night birds, no rustling of small creatures in the brush. Even the wind seems to have died away. Ahead, the path dips into a shallow valley. You can make out your destination: a sunken stone stairway cut into the hillside. Even from this distance, you can see the debris scattered around the entrance—broken stone, splintered wood, and the dark mouth of the tomb itself yawning open like a wound in the earth. Recent footprints mark the disturbed ground around the entrance, though whoever made them is long gone. Where do you go? - Enter the tomb - Return to the village
[Player] I light a torch and enter the tomb
[GM] You pull out a torch from your pack and strike flint to steel. The flame catches, casting dancing shadows across the disturbed entrance. Broken stone seals and scattered debris crunch under your boots as you descend the sunken steps. The torch reveals ancient stonework—fitted blocks worn smooth by centuries. The air grows colder still as you move deeper, your breath now visible in the flickering light. The entrance opens into a low chamber, forcing you to duck slightly as you enter. Faded murals cover the walls, their colors dulled by age. In the wavering torchlight, you can make out scenes of a crowned figure receiving homage from kneeling subjects—clearly a king of some ancient realm. But something is wrong with the artwork. Every depiction of the royal figure has had its eyes violently scratched out, the stone gouged deep by metal tools. The damage looks recent. Dust and broken pottery litter the floor, kicked aside by the passage of the treasure-seekers who came before you. A single corridor leads deeper into the tomb, disappearing into darkness beyond your torch's reach. The silence down here is absolute, broken only by the soft crackle of your flame and the whisper of your own breathing. Where do you go? - Deeper into the tomb
[Player] I move slowly toward the corridor
[GM] You advance cautiously down the corridor, torch held high. The flame illuminates stone walls lined with rectangular niches—burial alcoves that once held the king's retainers and guards. Most are empty now, containing only dust and bone fragments. But one niche still holds its occupant. As your torchlight falls upon it, a skeletal figure in rusted chainmail steps down from its resting place. Ancient metal scrapes against stone as it moves. A corroded shortsword gleams dully in its bony grip, and empty eye sockets seem to track your movement. The skeleton warrior raises its blade and advances with mechanical precision, mail links rattling with each step. Wisps of cold mist curl around its bones—the same unnatural chill that has been plaguing the village. This tomb's first defender has awakened to bar your passage. The undead guardian moves to attack! What do you do?
