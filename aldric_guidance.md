# Aldric Ironhand — Character Notes

## Role

Aldric is the Gauntlet's test pregen. He is the character you push through the hub when you need to walk the rules engine end-to-end and confirm nothing has regressed. Narrative depth is secondary to mechanical coverage — the profile below exists so the GM has a consistent voice to narrate in when the tests run.

## Appearance

Early thirties. Broad-shouldered, dark-haired, grey-eyed, clean-shaven in the military way. A long white scar runs from the point of the jaw down onto the side of the neck — the kind of wound that should have ended a life and did not. Carries himself with the discipline of someone who was, and is no longer, an officer. Chain mail is well-kept; longsword is plain; the only piece of his kit that is not regulation is the copper ring he wears on a cord around his neck, which he does not discuss.

## Personality

Formal. Direct. Speaks in complete sentences and expects the same. Thanks a quartermaster by name. Does not swear in mixed company. There is a slight stiffness to the manners that suggests they were trained into him by someone who used a rod; he has kept them past the point of usefulness because at this point he does not know any others.

Aldric is a veteran of the **Eastward Wars** — a border campaign against a neighboring baronial holding some eight years ago — and he commanded a small garrison under a superior whose orders, in the last months, became indefensible. The decision Aldric made at the end of that campaign is the reason he is no longer an officer and the reason he wanders. He does not discuss it. The copper ring was given to him by a corporal in his command who did not survive that decision.

He is not bitter. He is not cruel. He is careful, now, in a way he was not then.

## Backstory

Born in **Oreton**, a garrison town on the Inner Marches. Enlisted at seventeen. Rose to lieutenant by twenty-two, captain by twenty-six, commanded the Fort Belden garrison by twenty-eight. Resigned the commission at thirty, under unclear circumstances. Has been a wandering sword-for-hire since, taking work that is narrow enough in scope that he does not need to make the kind of decisions he made at Fort Belden.

He arrived at the Gauntlet because a Warden-adjacent priest at a chapel two days' ride south recognized something in him and suggested he walk the old training hall beneath the ruins to the north. The priest did not say why. Aldric did not press. He descended.

## Class and play notes

**Fighter, Champion Archetype, Level 3.** Mechanically identical to Ren — high STR, defensive fighting style, Second Wind and Action Surge, 19–20 crit threat. The distinction is flavor. When running test sessions through the Gauntlet, use Aldric rather than Ren so the Crow's Hollow pregen stays clean of test-state contamination.

**Starting equipment.** Steel longsword, chain mail, iron shield. No magic items; the Oathblade is the in-module pickup. Aldric's inventory is intentionally spare: the engine should have to grant, equip, and apply the magic bonuses of a wholly new weapon over the course of the test walk.

**Second Wind** is the test-hub healing fallback. Burn it liberally in the Gauntlet — if the engine's pool decrement or the short-rest recharge is broken, this is the chamber it will show up.

**Action Surge** fires once, at the Black Gate Warden, as the scripted test of the one-extra-action path. If the engine doesn't grant a second attack (or doesn't decrement the pool), that's a bug to file.

## Voice in play

Short declarative sentences, with officer-cadence. "Clear the threshold first." "Hold position; I'll check the floor." "The mist is acrid — cloth at the mouth."

When addressed in the second person by the GM narration, Aldric responds formally. Refers to himself and others by rank when under stress. Addresses NPCs as *sir* or *ma'am* by default and will be correct about it even when the NPC is a talking skeleton. That last detail is funnier than he intends.

## Things Aldric cares about, in order

1. Discipline, specifically his own.
2. Not repeating the decision he made at Fort Belden.
3. The copper ring.
4. The corporal whose name is engraved on the inside of the ring.
5. Walking the Gauntlet cleanly, chamber by chamber, in the order his old training would have sent him through.

## Things Aldric is uneasy about

- Orders from people he does not know.
- Lantern-work he cannot explain.
- Children asking him about Fort Belden.
- Silence in a room that should have a pulse in it.

(The Gauntlet is mostly the last one.)
