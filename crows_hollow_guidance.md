# The Watch at Crow's Hollow — GM Running Notes

## The heart of the module

Lt. Orick Havel was buried alive. His own company sealed him in the crypt in a panic during the Falling Winter, sixty years ago, before he had finished dying. The Watch's closing ritual — the phrase the player will speak at the wardstone lantern — was supposed to be said *over him* to put him properly to rest. It never was. The ritual's interrupted weight is what anchors his spirit here, what keeps the watch-lanterns lighting themselves, and what is, very slowly, pulling a wight together out of the Lieutenant's bones.

This isn't a module about killing a monster. It's a module about finishing a ritual that somebody else didn't finish. The violence is optional. The finishing is not.

Honor that in your narration: the whole watch-house is the ritual the players have walked into the middle of.

## Pacing — 75 to 90 minutes

- 10 minutes: yard, gate room, establishing the setting and the mystery of the lanterns.
- 15 minutes: barracks fight. First real combat. Set the tone.
- 20 minutes: officer's study fight + searching + puzzle. The journal is the pivot.
- 5 minutes: back to the lantern room, saying the phrase, opening the crypt.
- 15–25 minutes: the crypt. Encounter resolution (fight, ritual, or both).
- 5 minutes: coda. Fade to the village.

If the player is faster than this, excellent. If slower, let them take their time — especially in the officer's study. Finding the journal is the module's spine.

## Combat calibration — you're tuned for the Lanternblade

The pregen character carries the Lanternblade. The boss is built with radiant vulnerability specifically so that blade lands properly. If the player has somehow lost, pawned, or not-drawn the Lanternblade, telegraph its importance during the descent from the lantern room — the blade's runes warm visibly as the crypt opens, the greenish light recoils when the blade is unsheathed. If the player still refuses to draw it, consider softening Havel (reduce HP by 10–15, grant the player inspiration-style bonus for their stubbornness) rather than simply killing them.

Conversely: don't let the blade trivialize the fight. Havel has enough HP and enough initiative to make a level-3 player sweat even with the Lanternblade in hand. He will get two or three good swings in; expect the player to drop below half HP at least once. The *wounded* condition almost certainly ends up active.

## The narrative-resolution path

If the player figures out that Havel is looking for someone to finish the ritual — and at any point says the closing phrase aloud at him, or offers to do the laying-to-rest — **this resolves the encounter without killing him**. He lowers the sword, walks back to the sarcophagus, lies down, and goes still. The greenish light pinches out. XP, treasure, and completion all fire as normal.

Players who spot this path deserve full recognition. A well-run version of this module ends not with the Lieutenant's corpse on the flags but with the Lieutenant's coat folded neatly in the sarcophagus and the player blowing out the wardstone lantern on the way out.

## The closing-phrase-as-spell trick

During combat, the player may try to speak the closing phrase at Havel. The Closing Phrase Vulnerability ability handles this: it's a standard action, it provokes a WIS save at DC 12, and on a failure it does 2d8 radiant damage and costs him his next turn. Lean into this. A player who realizes the phrase is a weapon and commits to speaking it at him between swings is playing the module the way it's designed to be played — reward them.

## NPCs and the coda

The module doesn't strictly require an NPC, but the hook — a child from Thornford's Common went into the watch-house and didn't come back — implies one. The GM should mention the child (her name is **Peri**, she's about eleven, she's the daughter of the village lamplighter) in the yard description if asked, and should plant her in the barracks as a non-hostile discovery if the player thinks to look: she hid in one of the untaken bunks when the spirit rose and has been silent since. She's fine, mostly. She wants to go home.

If Peri is found and escorted out, the coda at Thornford's Common is warm rather than cold. If she is not found — the player blew past the barracks, didn't search — the coda is quieter, and the player will learn about Peri from the lamplighter, who has been sitting at his shop window for three days waiting. Both endings are legitimate; don't punish the fast player.

## The gift

On return to Thornford's Common with the Lieutenant properly resolved, the lamplighter — a retired Warden-adjacent cleric named **Matthew of the Split Oak** — will recognize the cloak-pin the lockbox contained (if the player took it) and offer, in trade, a quiet blessing, a night's lodging, and a single healing potion per rest for the duration of the player's stay in the region. If the player returns the Rubbing of the Lantern Tree to the village shrine, narrate villagers removing their caps.

If the player killed Havel rather than ritually resolving him, the coda is still warm but Matthew's face is tight when he hears the account. Leave a small sorrow in the scene. The Hollowmarch is a country where how you do the work matters.

## Rules-pack-specific notes

- **Advantage/disadvantage** is on in Lantern & Blade. Use it in the crypt: Havel's reading of Warden tactics grants him advantage against any player who disrespects the Watch (attacking from behind the sarcophagus, desecrating the gear, etc.). Conversely, a player who speaks Warden words, carries Warden gear, or otherwise *belongs* gets advantage on their first round's attack.
- **The wounded condition** should almost certainly be active by the crypt. Narrate it into the approach — the player limps down the stairs, sets a palm on the wall. Havel notices. An officer always notices an injured soldier.
- **Encumbrance** (15 × STR) rarely matters in this module. The Lanternblade's 3 weight slots, chain mail's 55, and the character's pack are the main weights; a STR 12 or higher carries everything. Note it if a player loots beyond capacity.

## Things to withhold, things to reveal

**Withhold:** that Havel was buried alive until the player examines the sarcophagus interior. That the phrase is the resolution until the player finds the journal. The precise mechanics of the Unfinished ability — let the player discover the phrase-as-weapon through play.

**Reveal freely:** that the Wardens are the ritual order the watch-house belongs to. That Havel was an officer here. That the lanterns' relighting is not normal and is tied to whatever is in the crypt. Villagers should know most of this shape if asked. The Hollowmarch is well-rumored country.
