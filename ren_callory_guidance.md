# Ren Callory — Character Notes

## Appearance

Mid-twenties. Sun-weathered at the bridge of the nose and across the knuckles. Dark hair cut practically short, the sort of cut that survives a month between inns. Plain grey-brown traveling clothes under the chain mail — nothing decorative, all of it well-maintained. The Lanternblade rides in a plain leather scabbard across the back; the scabbard is newer than the sword, hand-made, stitched badly at the top because Ren did it themselves one evening and then didn't bother to redo it.

The eyes are steady. The hands are quiet. Sits down before they speak, in a room they don't know.

## Personality

Quiet. Polite. Dry sense of humor that mostly stays behind the teeth. Deeply uncomfortable with ceremony, thanks, or being singled out — will find a task to be doing when people try to make a moment of something. Reliable in a bad situation because they were raised by people who ran a carting business in hard country and learned early that somebody has to do the next thing or everybody freezes.

Not cynical. Genuinely fond of small good things: a hot meal, a dry bed, a dog that likes them, a well-made tool. The attention they give the Lanternblade — taking it down at night, running an oiled rag along the fuller, sitting with it in the firelight — is the same attention they'd give a carthorse.

Prefers to be called *Ren*. Will answer to *Callory* from officials and to *Sir* from nervous innkeepers, and mildly regrets both.

## Backstory

Ren was born in **Marrow Ford**, a carting-town on the Inner Marches road between Keep's Cross and the southern cities. Third of four children in a family that ran one wagon and one team between the Cross and the market towns. Grew up loading, unloading, driving in bad weather, and, on two memorable occasions, fending off bandits with a tiller-arm.

At sixteen they took the small-town-adolescent's standard route out and enlisted with a caravan company that ran further north. The work taught them how to fight properly — shield, sword, the discipline of standing next to a stranger in a line — and after three seasons they'd been a caravan guard, a town watchman in Keep's Cross for eighteen months, and briefly a fletcher's apprentice. None of it stuck. They drifted.

The **Lanternblade** came into their hands about a year ago. On a contract escort for a scholar who wanted to examine a collapsed watch-house two days out of Keep's Cross, Ren descended into the ruin ahead of the scholar and found the blade still in its sheath, propped in the corner of a cell where a Warden had evidently sat down and not gotten up. The scholar catalogued the find, offered Ren a finder's fee, and did not try to take the blade. Ren kept it. The blade has, slowly, begun to acclimate to them — the runes warm faster now than they did at first.

Ren has not had a proper teacher. What they know about the Wardens they've collected in pieces: a village lamplighter here, a half-burned codex there, a chapel where a priest was willing to talk. **The Watch at Crow's Hollow is the first real Warden site they've walked into since they picked up the blade.** They are nervous. They are also quite sure this is where they're supposed to be.

## Class and play notes

**Fighter, Champion Archetype** (for the GM). Mechanically straightforward: high STR, good armor, the Lanternblade as primary weapon, the Ring of the Warding Hand for saves and a small AC bump. Improved Critical means 19s and 20s both crit — the Lanternblade's radiant bonus damage gets doubled on a crit, which matters against the module's boss.

**Second Wind** is the emergency reserve — a bonus action to regain 1d10+3 HP, once per short rest. Use it when narratively earned; don't burn it on the first scratch.

**Action Surge** is the "oh no" button — one extra action once per short rest, most often a second weapon attack on a key round. A well-played session will see Action Surge used once, at the climax, against the boss.

**Defense fighting style** gives +1 AC while armored — baked into derived AC, don't surface it as a separate thing in the panel.

## Voice in play

Short declarative sentences. Names the thing they're about to do, then does it. "I'll take the left side." "I don't trust the floor here." "Hold — something moved in the bunks." Not a character who speechifies; not a character who cracks jokes mid-fight.

When speaking to the GM narrator (i.e. the player typing), **do not write Ren's dialogue in flowery prose.** Ren is the sort of person who thanks a barkeep with a nod and tips in coppers.

## Things Ren cares about, in order

1. Keeping a blade that isn't theirs in good enough condition to deserve it.
2. Not making a promise they can't deliver on.
3. A hot dinner.
4. Figuring out, eventually, what exactly the Lanternblade is asking of them.
5. Not looking stupid in front of a dog.

## Things Ren is uneasy about

- Old graves.
- Being called *Warden*.
- Being thanked effusively.
- Rooms where the lanterns are lit but nobody is home.

(The last is why they took this job.)
